Decentralized Machine Learning: Verification Challenges

Decentralized networks promise to democratize AI by pooling consumer-grade GPU resources. However, scaling training across untrusted nodes introduces a major security bottleneck: verification.

How do we verify that a node actually performed the forward and backward passes correctly, rather than submitting random noise or malicious gradients?

One approach is Proof of Learning. Nodes submit periodic checkpoints, and validators re-run small slices of the computation to verify matching states. However, floating-point non-determinism across different GPU architectures makes exact verification difficult.

Another frontier is Zero-Knowledge Machine Learning (ZKML). By generating cryptographic proofs of execution, a node can prove mathematical correctness without the validator re-running the model. While current ZK proving overhead is high, optimization is moving rapidly, representing a key event horizon for open, decentralized intelligence.
